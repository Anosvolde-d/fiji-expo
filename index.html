Culture & Heritage

Fiji is home to a rich blend of indigenous Fijian, Indian, and other Pacific cultures. The traditional "Bula" greeting embodies the warm, friendly spirit of the Fijian people.

beach_access

Natural Beauty

With crystal-clear waters, vibrant coral reefs, and lush tropical forests, Fiji is one of the world's most beautiful destinations. The islands offer stunning beaches and incredible marine biodiversity.

translate

Languages

English, Fijian, and Fiji Hindi are the official languages. English is widely spoken, making it accessible for international visitors and students.

TRADITIONAL OUTFITS

Ceremonial Clothing

Fijian traditional attire reflects deep cultural pride. The sulu, a wraparound garment, is worn daily and at ceremonies. For weddings and special occasions, intricate tapa cloth and masi designs showcase ancestral artistry passed down through generations.

WHERE IS FIJI?

Fiji is an archipelago of more than 330 islands in the heart of the South Pacific Ocean, about 2,000 km north of New Zealand. Situated on the 180th meridian, Fiji is one of the first nations to greet each new day.
